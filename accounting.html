F.A.Q

Frequently Asked Questions

To help you - our customers quickly and easily find answers to commonly asked questions, saving them time and improving their overall experience with us.

  • What are KRA revenue returns?

    In Kenya, Employment Returns refer to the tax returns that employers are required to file with the Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA) to report their employees' income tax and other statutory deductions.

  • What Do I Need To Have To File Employment Returns?

    To file Employment Returns in Kenya, you need to have the necessary information and documentation, such as your employees' personal details, their income and benefits, and records of taxes and statutory deductions withheld from their salaries. It's recommended to consult with a tax professional or the KRA for specific requirements and guidelines.

  • What Is Tax Relief In Employment Returns?

    , tax relief refers to the deductions that can be claimed by employees and employers to reduce their taxable income and lower their tax liability. Examples of tax relief in Kenya include personal relief, insurance relief, and pension relief. It's important to note that different types of tax relief have specific eligibility criteria and conditions, so it's advisable to consult with a tax professional Like Jowa Kirogo and Associates or the Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA) for guidance.

  • When Am I Supposed To File My Employment Returns?

    In Kenya, employers are required to file their Employment Returns with the Kenya Revenue Authority (KRA) on or before the 9th day of the following month. For example, the Employment Returns for the month of January should be filed on or before the 9th of February. It's important to note that failure to file Employment Returns on time may result in penalties and interest charges, so it's recommended to submit them well before the deadline.

  • What incentives for Corporation Tax are available to recently listed companies?

    As of 25th April 2020, The Tax Laws (Amendment) Act, 2020 removed the provisions in the Income Tax Act that provided preferential tax treatment to newly listed companies. Currently, there are no special tax incentives available for such companies under the Income Tax Act.

  • What is a waiver application?

    If taxpayers in Kenya fail to pay their taxes or file returns by the specified deadlines, they may be subject to penalties and interest charges as per the law. These penalties and interest are considered as taxes payable. However, taxpayers can request the Commissioner to waive such penalties and interest. It's important to note that this provision only applies to penalties and interest, and the principal tax must be paid in full. The process of applying for the remission of penalties and interest is known as a waiver application, which the Commissioner may approve fully or partially based on the justification and evidence provided. It's worth noting that penalties and interest charged under fraud or tax evasion are not eligible for waiver consideration.
